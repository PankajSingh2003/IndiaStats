Hey, We are Devs

The objective of this project is to conduct a comprehensive analysis of India's developmental achievements across various critical sectors, including nominal and PPP GDP, DRDO establishments, arable land, steel production, and other key indicators. The project is centered around web development, and users will be presented with options to choose specific areas of India's progress that they would like to explore in detail. The goal is to provide accurate and informative insights into India's growth trajectory and highlight the country's notable accomplishments in key areas.

DRDO Centres

The Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) has various research centers located throughout India, each with a specific focus area. These centers are responsible for the development of technology for the Indian armed forces. Some of the prominent centers include Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) for aeronautical technologies, Defence Metallurgical Research Laboratory (DMRL) for metallurgical technologies, and Defence Research and Development Laboratory (DRDL) for missile technologies. Other centers focus on avionics, electronic systems, chemical and biological warfare, and more. These centers play a critical role in strengthening India's defense capabilities through the development of state-of-the-art technologies.

Arable Land

India has a total land area of 328 million hectares, out of which about 157.35 million hectares are classified as agricultural land. This agricultural land is distributed across the country, with the largest share in the states of Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and Madhya Pradesh. The country's agriculture sector is diverse, with a wide range of crops grown including wheat, rice, pulses, oilseeds, cotton, sugarcane, fruits, and vegetables.

Steel Production

India is the world's second-largest producer of steel after China. The Indian steel industry has witnessed significant growth in recent years, with production increasing from 81.2 million tonnes in 2013 to 111.2 million tonnes in 2019. The industry is dominated by large integrated steel producers such as Tata Steel, JSW Steel, and Steel Authority of India Limited (SAIL). The government has launched initiatives such as the National Steel Policy to increase the production capacity of the sector and promote domestic consumption.

Average Rainfall

India is a diverse country with varied climatic conditions. The average rainfall per state in India varies significantly due to its geographical and topographical features. The states in the northeastern region of India such as Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, and Assam receive the highest amount of rainfall in the country, with an average annual rainfall of more than 2500 mm. On the other hand, states like Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Haryana in the western region receive very low rainfall, with an average annual rainfall of less than 500 mm. The southern states of Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Andhra Pradesh receive moderate rainfall, ranging from 600-1500 mm. The central region states like Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh receive an average annual rainfall of around 1100-1300 mm.

Gross Expenditure on Research

India's gross expenditure on research and development (GERD) has been steadily increasing in recent years, reflecting the government's commitment to promoting scientific and technological innovation. According to the National Science and Technology Management Information System (NSTMIS), the GERD in India for the fiscal year 2020-21 was Rs. 1,24,440 crore, an increase of 5.4% from the previous year. The majority of this funding comes from the government sector, with the private sector contributing a smaller but growing share. Despite this growth, India's GERD as a percentage of GDP remains relatively low compared to other countries, highlighting the need for continued investment in research and development to drive economic growth and innovation in the country.
